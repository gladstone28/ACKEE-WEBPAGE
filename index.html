History and Cultural Significance of Ackee

The ackee, originally from West Africa, has become a staple in Jamaican cuisine. It's famously known for being part of Jamaica's national dish, Ackee and Saltfish.

Through the years, ackee has woven itself into the fabric of Jamaican culture. It represents more than just a food item; it's a symbol of Jamaican heritage and resilience.

Discover the stories behind ackee's journey to Jamaica, its adoption into local cuisine, and how it has influenced the lives of people across the island.

Nutritional Information

Discover the nutritional benefits of ackee. Rich in protein, fiber, and essential vitamins, ackee is a healthy addition to any diet.

Learn about the health benefits and essential precautions necessary for preparing and consuming ackee to avoid toxicity.

Ackee is rich in nutrients. Here's a breakdown per 100g serving:

  • Calories: 151
  • Protein: 2.9g
  • Fat: 15g
  • Carbohydrates: 3.9g
  • Fiber: 2.7g
  • Vitamin C: 30mg

Note: Always ensure ackee is properly ripened and prepared to avoid toxicity.
